## Unique Value Proposition

Restaurant Bandung is one of the few Indonesian restaurants in the Netherlands that specializes specifically in West Javanese cuisine rather than the generic pan-Indonesian menu most competitors offer. Founded by Yana and Steven out of personal frustration that the authentic flavors they grew up with were disappearing from Dutch dining, the restaurant has earned a reputation as one of Den Bosch's top-rated eateries (4.5/5 TripAdvisor, #25 of 300 restaurants). The intimate, family-run atmosphere creates a dining experience that regulars describe as "the best Indonesian I have tasted in the Netherlands." Their rijsttafel (rice table) offering -- with five distinct variations including vegetarian and seafood options -- is the centerpiece of the menu.

## Founder / Team Background

**Yana & Steven** -- husband and wife team who own and operate the restaurant. They conceived the idea around 2016 because they "could hardly find the authentic flavors they grew up with anywhere" in the Netherlands. After searching for the right location, they found a characteristic building on Hinthamereinde in late 2016 and opened shortly after.

Their cuisine is specifically influenced by **West Javanese (Sundanese) cooking traditions**, which distinguishes it from the more common Javanese-Central or pan-Indonesian kitchens in the Netherlands. West Javanese cuisine is known for fresh vegetable preparations, complex sambals, and lighter coconut-based sauces compared to the heavier Central Javanese style.

## Target Audiences

### Primary
**Local Den Bosch residents (30--60)** who appreciate authentic cuisine and support neighborhood restaurants. These are repeat customers -- the review data shows strong loyalty patterns. They value quality ingredients, genuine flavors, and personal service over trendy presentation. Mid-to-upper income, willing to spend EUR 30--40 per person for a rijsttafel evening.

### Secondary
- **Indonesian-Dutch community** -- seeking flavors that remind them of home; the "we couldn't find our own flavors" origin story resonates strongly with this audience
- **Food tourists and day-trippers** visiting Den Bosch (a popular tourist destination) who search TripAdvisor/Google for highly-rated local restaurants
- **Vegetarian/vegan diners** -- the restaurant offers a dedicated vegetarian rijsttafel and is noted on vegan food blogs as accommodating plant-based requests
- **Catering clients** -- groups of 30+ people for events, requiring 2+ weeks advance notice

- What to learn: How to be culturally authentic without being cliche

### White Space (Unoccupied Territory)

The Indonesian restaurant web landscape in the Netherlands falls into two camps:
1. **Premium/Michelin tier** (Blauw, Ron Gastrobar) -- sleek, minimalist, expensive feel
2. **Ordering platform tier** (most others) -- functional, template-based, no atmosphere

Nobody occupies the **warm, intimate, family-story-driven middle ground**. A website that combines:
- The cultural authenticity and food photography quality of the premium tier
- The personal, family-run warmth and accessibility of a neighborhood restaurant
- A specific West Javanese identity (not generic "Indonesian")

This is Bandung's territory to claim.
